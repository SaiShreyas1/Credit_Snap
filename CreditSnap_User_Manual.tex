\chapter{Introduction}

\section{Purpose of this Manual}
This manual teaches students and canteen owners how to use every feature of CreditSnap. No technical knowledge is required to read it. The text explains each button and menu on the platform.

\section{Intended Audience}
This document serves two groups:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Students:} Users with an @iitk.ac.in email who want to order food and manage their credit.
    \item \textbf{Canteen Owners:} Shopkeepers who manage orders, menus, and student debts.
\end{itemize}

\section{Scope of CreditSnap}
CreditSnap is built for the IITK campus. It digitizes the paper khata system. Students can order food to arrive later or dine-in right away. Payment options include immediate payment via Razorpay or adding the total to a local limit. Owners accept or reject incoming orders and manage a live digital ledger.

\chapter{Getting Started}

\section{The Two User Roles}
The main screen has two tabs. Students use the "Student" tab to log in. Canteen owners use the "Shopkeeper" tab to access their controls. These two roles show completely different dashboards and tools.

\section{Student Registration}
To create a new account:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Step 1:} Go to the CreditSnap URL.
    \item \textbf{Step 2:} Click "Sign Up" on the login page.
    \item \textbf{Step 3:} Fill in the form. Provide your Full Name (cannot be blank), IITK Email (must end in @iitk.ac.in), Hall Number, Roll Number, Phone Number (10 digits), Password, and Confirm Password.
    \item \textbf{Step 4:} Click the "Sign Up" button.
    \item \textbf{Step 5:} A verification email is sent to your IITK email address.
    \item \textbf{Step 6:} Open the email and click "Verify Account".
    \item \textbf{Step 7:} Your account is now active. You can log in.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Email Verification}
\begin{notebox}
If the email does not arrive within 5 minutes, check the spam folder. To resend it: go to the login page, try to log in, and click "Resend Verification Email" when prompted.
\end{notebox}

\section{Student Login}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Step 1:} Go to the CreditSnap URL.
    \item \textbf{Step 2:} Click the "Student" tab.
    \item \textbf{Step 3:} Enter your @iitk.ac.in email and password.
    \item \textbf{Step 4:} Click "Login". You are taken to the Student Home Dashboard.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Canteen Owner Login}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Step 1:} Go to the CreditSnap URL.
    \item \textbf{Step 2:} Click the "Shopkeeper" tab.
    \item \textbf{Step 3:} Enter the email and password given to you by the platform administrator.
    \item \textbf{Step 4:} Click "Login". You are taken to the Active Orders Dashboard.
\end{enumerate}
\begin{notebox}
Canteen owner accounts are created by the system administrator. There is no public sign-up option for owners.
\end{notebox}

\section{Forgot Password}
To reset a lost password:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Step 1:} On the login page, click "Forgot Password?".
    \item \textbf{Step 2:} Enter your registered email address.
    \item \textbf{Step 3:} Click "Send Reset Link".
    \item \textbf{Step 4:} Open the email and click the reset link.
    \item \textbf{Step 5:} Enter your new password and confirm it.
    \item \textbf{Step 6:} Click "Confirm". Your password is updated. You can now log in with the new password.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Session Timeout}
If you do not interact with the website for 30 minutes, you are automatically logged out. This protects your account on shared or public computers. You will be taken back to the login page and asked to log in again.

\section{How to Log Out}
For students: click your name or avatar in the top-right corner of the page, then click "Logout".
For canteen owners: click the profile icon next to your canteen name in the header, then click "Logout".

\subsection{Current Orders Section}
This shows all your active orders. Each order card shows: the items you ordered, the quantities, the total price, the canteen name, and a status badge (Pending, Accepted, Rejected, or Ready). The status updates automatically without refreshing the page.

\subsection{Notification Bell}
Click the bell icon in the header to see your recent alerts, such as order accepted, order rejected, or credit limit warnings.

\section{Canteen Selection Page}
Click "Canteens" in the sidebar. You will see a list of all campus canteens. Each canteen card shows the canteen name, operating hours, how much you currently owe that canteen, and an Open/Closed badge. Click a canteen card to open its menu.

\section{Menu Page and Ordering}

\subsection{Browsing the Menu}
Items are organized into Veg, Non-Veg, and Beverages. Each item shows its name, price in ₹, and category. Items that are out of stock are grayed out and cannot be added to the cart.

\subsection{Adding Items to Cart}
Click "+" next to any available item to add it. Click "+" again to increase the quantity. Click "−" to reduce it. The cart summary on the side updates the total price as you add or remove items.

\subsection{Special Instructions}
Before placing the order, you can type a short note in the "Special Instructions" box (for example, "No onions" or "Extra spicy"). This note is sent to the canteen owner with your order.

\subsection{Choosing Order Mode}
Select one:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Pre-Order:} You are placing the order in advance and will arrive at the canteen in 10 to 40 minutes.
    \item \textbf{Dine-In:} You are already at the canteen or arriving immediately.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Choosing Payment Method}
Select one:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Pay Now:} You will pay immediately through the Razorpay payment gateway. The payment is processed and the order goes to the owner without any credit involvement.
    \item \textbf{Add to Credit:} The amount is added to your khata at this canteen.
\end{itemize}

\begin{warningbox}
The system first checks if your current debt plus this order total exceeds your credit limit. If it does, the "Add to Credit" option is disabled and a warning message is shown. You must clear some of your existing debt before you can use credit again.
\end{warningbox}

\subsection{Placing the Order}
Click "Confirm Order". The order is sent to the canteen owner and appears in your Current Orders with a "Pending" status.

\section{Order Tracking}
After placing an order, go to the Home Dashboard to track it.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Pending:} The owner has not acted on the order yet.
    \item \textbf{Accepted:} The owner accepted the order. Your debt is updated. The canteen prepares your food.
    \item \textbf{Rejected:} The owner rejected the order. You are not charged. You can place a new order.
    \item \textbf{Ready:} Your food is ready for pickup at the counter.
\end{itemize}

\begin{tipbox}
You will receive a notification every time your order status changes, so you do not need to keep checking manually.
\end{tipbox}

\section{View Debts Page}
Click "View Debts" in the sidebar. This page shows how much you owe each canteen. It provides a breakdown per canteen and a total at the top.

\subsection{Paying a Debt}

\begin{notebox}
Note for Evaluators: The Razorpay payment gateway is currently running in Test Mode for this project submission. Please do not use real credit card or bank details. A standard Razorpay test card can be used to simulate a successful transaction.
\end{notebox}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Step 1:} Find the canteen card for the debt you want to pay.
    \item \textbf{Step 2:} Click "Pay Now" on that card.
    \item \textbf{Step 3:} The Razorpay payment window opens. Complete the payment using UPI, card, or net banking.
    \item \textbf{Step 4:} Once the payment is successful, your debt for that canteen is reduced or cleared.
    \item \textbf{Step 5:} A record of this payment is added to your History page.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Partial Payment}
You do not have to pay the full amount at once. Change the amount in the payment box to pay a partial amount. Your debt will be reduced by however much you pay.

\subsection{No Debts}
If you have no outstanding debts, the page shows "No Debts Found" and the Pay Now buttons are disabled.

\subsection{Payment Failure}
If the payment gateway fails, an error message is shown. Your debt is not affected. You can try again.

\section{Order History Page}
Click "History" in the sidebar. This page shows all your past completed orders and cleared debts. For each entry you can see: the date, item names and quantities, total amount, payment method, and canteen name.

\section{Student Profile Management}

\subsection{Viewing Profile}
Click your name or avatar in the header to open your profile.

\subsection{Editing Profile}
Click "Edit". You can change your Name and Phone Number. Click "Save" to save the changes.
\begin{warningbox}
Your email address and roll number cannot be changed. If you try to save with a blank name or a phone number that is not exactly 10 digits, the system will block the save and show an error.

\subsection{Logging Out from Profile}
Click "Logout" from the profile menu to log out.

\chapter{Credit Ledger System}

\section{What is the Credit Ledger System}
The traditional system used physical paper notebooks to record debts. CreditSnap replaces this with a digital record. Every student has a separate balance at each canteen.

\section{How the Debt Ceiling Works}
Each owner restricts total credit through a limit. The default limit is ₹2500. If current debt plus a new order costs more than this limit, the "Add to Credit" option is blocked. A message explains that the limit is reached and partial payment is required.

\section{How Debt is Updated}
A student's debt reaches a higher number only when the canteen owner clicks "Accept" on a new purchase. If the order is rejected, no debt is added. Debts are never changed without owner action.

\section{The Immutable Ledger Policy}
Once an order is accepted, the transaction record is permanent. No user can delete or modify a completed transaction. This blocks disputes and ensures exact accuracy across the system.

\section{What Happens When the Limit is Exceeded}
The "Add to Credit" option turns gray. A warning message tells the student their current debt. It directs them to reduce the balance before ordering more.

\section{Billing Cycle}
A billing cycle is the time period set by the canteen owner. Debts are cleared at the end of this cycle. Owners inspect outstanding amounts and send reminders to the students holding balances.

\section{Partial Payments}
Students can pay any amount toward their debt. They do not have to pay the full balance at once. For example, if a student owes ₹1200, they can pay ₹500 now. Their debt becomes ₹700, which stays active until cleared.

\chapter{Security and Account Safety}

\section{IITK Email Restriction}
Only @iitk.ac.in email addresses can register as students. This ensures that only verified campus members can access the credit system. External domains are rejected completely.

\section{Email Verification}
New student accounts are inactive until the verification link is clicked. This verification process prevents fake accounts from passing through the system.

\section{Password Hashing}
Passwords are never stored as plain text. They are scrambled using bcrypt before being saved to the database. Even if the database were accessed by an outsider, the actual passwords cannot be read.

\section{JWT Session Tokens}
After you log in, the system gives your browser a token (JWT). This token verifies your identity on every page you visit. It expires automatically, leading to logout after 30 minutes of inactivity.

\section{HTTPS Encryption}
All data sent between your browser and the CreditSnap server is encrypted. No one on the local Wi-Fi network can see your web traffic or your payment details.

\section{Role-Based Access Control}
Students cannot open any owner features. Trying to load owner pages as a student will be blocked by the server. Students can only see their own records.

\section{Payment Security}
CreditSnap never keeps banking details on its disks. Personal bank data bypasses the database completely. All payment processing runs through the secure Razorpay pipeline.

\section{Session Timeout}
After 30 minutes of no activity, your session drops. You are logged out automatically on the screen.

\chapter{Troubleshooting and FAQs}

\noindent \textbf{I did not receive the verification email.} \\
Check your spam folder. If not there, go to the login page and click "Resend Verification Email".

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{I forgot my password.} \\
Click "Forgot Password?" on the login page and follow the steps to reset it via email.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{The "Add to Credit" option is grayed out.} \\
Your debt at this canteen has reached or exceeded the credit limit. Go to "View Debts" and pay off some of your balance before trying again.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{My order has been on "Pending" for a long time.} \\
The canteen owner has not accepted or rejected it yet. You will be notified as soon as they act on it.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{The payment gateway shows an error.} \\
The payment service may be temporarily unavailable. Wait a few minutes and try again. Your debt is not affected.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{Some menu items are grayed out.} \\
Those items have been marked as unavailable by the canteen owner. They are currently out of stock.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{I cannot save my profile changes.} \\
Make sure your name is not blank and your phone number is exactly 10 digits. Correct any errors and try again.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{A completed order is not appearing in my History.} \\
Only fully completed and paid or settled orders appear in History. Active or pending orders appear on the Home Dashboard.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{Owner: I searched for a student in Active Debts but got no results.} \\
Either the student has no outstanding balance at your canteen, or you may have typed their name or roll number incorrectly.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\noindent \textbf{Owner: The save failed when editing the menu.} \\
This is usually a temporary network issue. Try again. If it keeps failing, reload the page.
